# Review Governance

## Recommended separation of duties

Program owners may prepare and revise records. Evidence and methods reviewers assess distinct quality dimensions. Approvers make the release decision. Publishers execute publication and withdrawal after the gates pass.

A small organization may assign one person to more than one role, but the repository preserves which role and reviewer were used for each assignment and decision.

## Quality rubric

Quality dimensions are scored against an explicit maximum and weight. The repository normalizes the weighted result to 100 and assigns one of four descriptive grades:

- Excellent: 90–100
- Strong: 75–89.99
- Adequate: 60–74.99
- Weak: below 60

The score is a documented review judgment. It is not an audit opinion, certification, or guarantee that underlying evidence is true.

## Review comments

Comments remain open until explicitly resolved or marked `wont_fix`. An approval decision cannot be recorded while any comment in the assignment remains open.

## Decision history

New approval decisions supersede earlier decisions without deleting them. Conditions, rationale, reviewer identity, assignment, and decision time remain available in exports.
