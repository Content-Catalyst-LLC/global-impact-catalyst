# Claim Governance

Global Impact Catalyst separates a measurement from the claim made about it.

## Descriptive observation

States what was observed for an indicator in a period. It requires a documented source and method but makes no progress, comparison, contribution, or causal assertion.

## Progress-to-target statement

Relates the observation to a documented baseline and target using the direction-aware progress formula. It does not establish why progress occurred.

## Comparison

Compares a population, place, period, benchmark, or group. It requires an explicit comparison basis and a design capable of supporting comparison language.

## Contribution statement

States that an initiative plausibly contributed to an outcome. It requires an explicit rationale describing the mechanism, other contributors, and supporting evidence. Monitoring-only designs receive a caution warning.

## Causal claim

States that an initiative caused an outcome. Eligibility requires quasi-experimental or randomized design metadata, an explicit causal-design description, high confidence, and reviewed or published status. These checks are necessary but not sufficient for a defensible causal conclusion.
